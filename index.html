MADE WITH AUTHENTIC SOUTH INDIAN TRADITON

Malabar Paratha (also known as Kerala Parotta) is a soft, flaky, and layered flatbread originating from the Malabar region of Kerala, South India. Made from refined wheat flour, it is kneaded with oil and rolled into thin layers before being folded and cooked on a hot griddle with ghee or oil. Known for its rich texture and delightful crispiness outside with softness inside, Malabar Paratha is often served with spicy curries like chicken, mutton, or vegetable stew. It's a beloved dish across India for its unique taste and irresistible layers.
